Installation & setup

Installation

Do not have After Effects open during the installation.

1. Download Ink Drops In Motion from Studio Innate's website;

2. Download Displacer Pro from Plugin Everything's website. Do that by clicking on the "Download Free @ GUMROAD" button, input 0 in the price, Add to cart, set your email adress, and click get.

Alternatively, if you have an Aescripts account and use the Aescripts app, installing it that way is easier. If you do so, there's no need to install Displacer Pro manually like written below;

Windows

1. Extract both folders by right-clicking and pressing "Extract All", one at a time

2. Inside the Displacer Pro folder, navigate to the DisplacerPro.aex file for Windows and copy it;

3. Navigate to "Program Files\Adobe\Adobe After Effects <YEAR>\Support Files\Plug-ins" and paste the DisplacerPro.aex file;

4. Back in your downloads, inside the Ink Drops In Motion folder, copy all the files except the TUTORIAL.txt and the INK DROPS IN MOTION TEMPLATE files (meaning the the .jsx and the three .ffx files);

5. Navigate to "Program Files\Adobe\Adobe After Effects <YEAR>\Support Files\Scripts\ScriptUI Panels" and paste the four files there;

MacOS

1. Open both zip archives, one at a time;

2. Inside de Displacer Pro folder, navigate to the DisplacerPro.plugin file for macOS and copy it;

3. Navigate to "Applications/Adobe After Effects <YEAR>/Plug-ins" and paste the DisplacerPro.plugin file;

4. Back in your downloads, inside the Ink Drops In Motion folder, copy all the files except the TUTORIAL.txt and the INK DROPS IN MOTION TEMPLATE files (meaning the the .jsx and the three .ffx files);

5. Navigate to "Applications/Adobe After Effects <YEAR>/Scripts/ScriptUI Panels" and paste the four files there;

After Effects setup

1. Launch After Effects;

2. Go to "Preferences > Scripting & Expressions" and check "Allow Scripts to Write Files and Access Network".
This is in order to make the "Tutorial" button work;

3. Inside your composition, make sure to have a solid background: not the default background color.
To do this, make sure to have a solid covering all of the composition, or any other non-transparent layer;

4. Go to "Window" and scroll all the way down until you see Ink Drops In Motion 2.0, and click it;

5. Click on the "Let the ink drop!" button.
All the effect's settings are located on the IDIM FX layer in yellow.

Template file

If for some reason the "Let the ink drop!" button doesn't work properly, or if you simply prefer to use a template, you can use the template file.

Double-click on it, do your thing, save it. Keep the template file to be reused. You can of course change everything about it, like the composition size, framerate, bit depth, ...

Motion tab

Looping

When checked, the disruption will loop between the start and end point of your timeline.

This does not work on the properties affected by the audio-reactive features when they're enabled.

Automatic evolution

When checked, the effect will automatically animate over time. When unchecked, use the Manual evolution.

This setting is discarded when A-R evolution is enabled.

Speed of automatic evolution

Controls the speed of the evolution over time.

This is dependant on the lenght of the composition; a speed of 5 will be slower in a 30 seconds composition than in a 10 seconds composition.

This also affects the speed of the A-R evolution when it is enabled.

Manual evolution

When Automatic evolution is unchecked, Manual evolution is used to drive the evolution over time of the effect. Controlled using keyframes.

If you want your manual evolution to loop after a set amount of rotations, have Looping checked, and set the Speed of automatic evolution to the number of full rotations after which you want the evolution to loop.

If your Manual evolution is looped, say to 5 full rotations, going to 10 full rotations will make it loop twice.

This setting is discarded when A-R evolution is enabled.

Seed

Ink Drops In Motion is an effect randomly generated according to the seed. Change the seed to get a different randomly generated result ; all of your parameters will be maintained, but you will get a different result.

Map tab

Gamma (line thickness)

Dictates the thickness of each line. I recommend to leave this setting pretty low, unless you're using grain.

Size

Drives the scale of the effect. Not to be confused with the Displacement amount.

Size X/Y ratio

Controls how squished/stretched the effect is.

A number lower than 1 will give you a horizontally tight effect.

1 will get you a homogeneous ratio.

A number higher than 1 will give you a vertically tight effect.

Grain

Enables or disables grain.

Grain size

Drives the size of the grain. Around 20 with a high Grain strength makes for a good acid effect.

Grain strength

Controls how much the grain is visible. Play with this setting along with Gamma for great results.

Effects

Effects apply to the effect, not to your content.

Posterize

Enable or disable posterization.

Posterization

Drives how many separate "layers" the effect is split into.

Pixelise

Enable or disable pixelisation.

Pixel size X & Y

Drives the width (X) and height (Y) of each pixel.

Displacement tab

Displace X & Y

Controls how much the effect affects the content under, on each axis (X is horizontal, Y is vertical).

A number further from 0 will affect more the content under.

You can go into negative numbers.

Displace angle

Controls how much the effect affects the content under, on a rotational axis.

This means the effect affects less in the center than on the sides, though it stills gives a pretty organic result compared to the X and Y options.

Chromatic aberration

Controls the amount of chromatic aberration on each color channel (red, green and blue).

A higher number means that this color channel is less affected by the effect. 1 means the color channel is completely unaffected by the effect.

Combine the values to get interesting results, like Red 0.40, Green 0.20, Blue 0.00.

Instead of putting all three values to something higher than 0, simply decrease the Displacement amount. In other words, try to keep at least one of the three values to 0.

Iterations

Iterations blend between the effect and the original unaffected content in an organic way. Use it, especially with chromatic aberration, for smoother, fluid results.

Edge behavior

Controls how the edge of the video reacts when affected by the effect.

None will show the composition's background color.

Repeat will repeat the left part of the video over to the right, the bottop over to the top, ...

Mirror repeat will mirror the edge over itself.

Edge repeat will extend the last pixel next to the edge until the point where it is affected by the effect. Recommended.

Audio-reactive tab

1. Make sure you have at least one layer with audio enabled in your composition;

2. Click the "Allow for audio-reactive features" button.
Once this button is clikced, any changes in the composition's audio will not affect the effect.

Audio adjusting

Audio smoothing

This smoothes your audio (not auditively, just to the effect), to make it less jittery.

Audio contrast

Audio contrast type

None completely disables audio contrast.

Linear gets rid of the audio's influcence the more Audio contrast low is up, and maxes out the audio's influence the more Audio contrast high is down.

Lean towards low does the same as Linear, but pushes all of the middle values down a bit. This mean the sound will have to be very loud to have effect, otherwise it will have little effect, until it is just completely cut out by the Audio contrast low.

Lean both ways (increase contrast) does both Lean towards low and lean towards high at the same time.

Lean towards high does the same as Linear, but pushes all of the middle values up a bit. This mean the sound will have to be very quiet to not have effect, until it is just completely maxed out by the Audio contrast high.

Audio contrast low & high

Any value lower than Audio contrast low will be cut out, cutting the sound's influence on the effect.

Any value highrt than Audio contrast high will be maxed out, maxing the sound's influence on the effect.

Both are measured in positive decibels. To see the audio's waveform, open in your composition's timeline IDIM A-R Layer > Effects > IDIM Audio Keyframes > Slider, and click the "Show Post-Expression Graph" button (the small graph icon next to the = icon). Then, go into the Graph Editor.

A-R evolution

When turned on, let the loudness of the song drive the evolution of the effect.

The overall speed is controlled by the Speed of automatic evolution.

A-R displacement

When turned on, let the loudness of the song drive the displacement amount of the effect.

The Displacement amount controls the maximum displacement.

FAQ

Clicking "Let the ink drop!" creates the layers but doesn't apply the effects.

This shouldn't happen. Please contact me via Instagram at @glypse.design or email at hello@glypse.design. In the meantime, here is a quick fix:

1. Copy the three animation presets files (meaning the MAP , FX and A-R files) from your Ink Drops In Motion download folder;

2. Paste them inside the User Presets folder inside your documents folder:

macOS: /Documents/Adobe/After Effects <YEAR>/User Presets

Windows: Program Files\Adobe\Adobe After Effects <YEAR>\Support Files\User Presets\

3. Launch After Effects;

4. Inside the "Effects & Presets" panel, search "IDIM";

5. Select each of the layers generated and apply them their respective effect, one by one.

This will render the Audio adjusting broken.

The "Allow for audio-reactive features" doesn't work properly.

This might be because you are using an older version of After Effects and/or in a different language. This is most likely an easy fix; contact me via Instagram at @glypse.design or email at hello@glypse.design with your After Effects version and language.
