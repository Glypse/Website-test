Installation & setup

Installation

Do not have After Effects open during the installation.

1. Download Pixel Disruptor in Motion from Studio Innate's website;

2. Download Displacer Pro from Plugin Everything's website. Do that by clicking on the "Download Free @ GUMROAD" button, input 0 in the price, Add to cart, set your email adress, and click get.

Alternatively, if you have an Aescripts account and use the Aescripts app, installing it that way is easier. If you do so, there's no need to install Displacer Pro manually like written below;

Windows

1. Extract both folders by right-clicking and pressing "Extract All", one at a time

2. Inside the Displacer Pro folder, navigate to the DisplacerPro.aex file for Windows and copy it;

3. Navigate to "Program Files\Adobe\Adobe After Effects <YEAR>\Support Files\Plug-ins" and paste the DisplacerPro.aex file;

4. Back in your downloads, inside the Pixel Disruptor in Motion folder, copy all the files except the TUTORIAL.txt and the PXDiM TEMPLATE files (meaning the Pixel Disruptor ScriptUI, MAP X, MAP Y and Content effects files);

5. Navigate to "Program Files\Adobe\Adobe After Effects <YEAR>\Support Files\Scripts\ScriptUI Panels" and paste the four files there;

MacOS

1. Open both zip archives, one at a time;

2. Inside de Displacer Pro folder, navigate to the DisplacerPro.plugin file for macOS and copy it;

3. Navigate to "Applications/Adobe After Effects <YEAR>/Plug-ins" and paste the DisplacerPro.plugin file;

4. Back in your downloads, inside the Pixel Disruptor in Motion folder, copy all the files except the TUTORIAL.txt and the PXDiM TEMPLATE files (meaning the Pixel Disruptor ScriptUI, MAP X, MAP Y and Content effects files);

5. Navigate to "Applications/Adobe After Effects <YEAR>/Scripts/ScriptUI Panels" and paste the four files there;

After Effects setup

1. Launch After Effects;

2. Go to "Preferences > Scripting & Expressions" and check "Allow Scripts to Write Files and Access Network".
This is in order to make the "Tutorial" button work;

3. Inside your composition, make sure to have a solid background: not the default background color.
To do this, make sure to have a solid covering all of the composition, or any other non-transparent layer;

4. Go to "Window" and scroll all the way down until you see Pixel Disruptor <VERSION, ScriptUI, ...>, and click it;

5. Click on the "Disrupt your pixels!" button.
All the effect's settings are located on the PXD FX layer in yellow.

Template file

If for some reason the "Displace your pixels!" button doesn't work properly, or if you simply prefer to use a template, you can use the template file.

Double-click on it, do your thing, save it. Keep the template file to be reused. You can of course change everything about it, like the composition size, framerate, bit depth, ...

Motion tab

Looping

When checked, the disruption will loop between the start and end point of your timeline.

This does not work on the properties affected by the audio-reactive features when they're enabled.

Automatic evolution

When checked, the effect will automatically animate over time. When unchecked, use the Manual evolution.

Speed of automatic evolution

Controls the speed of the evolution over time.

This is dependant on the lenght of the composition; a speed of one will be slower in a 30 seconds composition than in a 10 seconds composition.

Manual evolution

When Automatic evolution is unchecked, Manual evolution is used to drive the evolution over time of the effect. Controlled using keyframes.

If you want your manual evolution to loop after a set amount of rotations, have Looping checked, and set the Speed of automatic evolution to the number of full rotations after which you want the evolution to loop.

If your Manual evolution is looped, say to 5 full rotations, going to 10 full rotations will make it loop twice.

Wiggle evolution group

Wiggles the evolution over time of the effect.

Refer to wiggles.

Seed

Pixel Disruptor in Motion is an effect randomly generated according to the seed. Change the seed to get a different randomly generated result ; all of your parameters will be maintained, but you will get a different result.

Map tab

Style

Drives the kind of output you will get;
Moving cells will put your animation inside lots of squares, and those squares will be moving around.
Changing cells will put your animation inside lots of squares, and the content of these squares will be moving around.

Contrast amplification

When set to a high number like 10, the movement of the squares will go from one extreme to the other extreme while going faster through the center (in terms of position).

When set to a low number like 0, the movement of the squares will go from one point to the other in a linear fashion.

Think of it as keyframes: a high number means a tighter easing, while a lower number means a more linear easing in between the positions.

Size

Drives the scale of the effect. Not to be confused with the Displacement amount.

Size X/Y ratio

Controls how squished/stretched the effect is.

A number lower than 1 will give you a horizontally tight effect.

1 will get you perfect squares.

A number higher than 1 will give you a vertically tight effect.

Complexity

Drives how complex the output is, not through time.

Wiggle complexity group

Wiggles the complexity over time of the effect.

Refer to wiggles.

Complexity influence

Controls how much each layer of complexity is visible. A low number will output a visual complexity near to 1, while a very high number will make it so that only the very complex layer of the effect is visible.

I recommend to keep between 50% and 80% for the expected output.

Partially disabling group

Partially disabling

When checked, the effect will affect certain parts of the image.

Disabling threshold

0-255 value. 128 means that only half of the image is affected by Pixel Disruptor. A higher number means less of the image is affected.

Disabling threshold

Wiggles the disabling threshold over time of the effect.

Refer to wiggles.

Displacement tab

Displacement amount (X and Y)

Controls how much the effect affects the content under, on each axis (X is horizontal, Y is vertical).

A number further from 0 will affect more the content under.

You can go into negative numbers.

Wiggle displacement group

Wiggles the displacement amount over time of the effect.

Refer to wiggles.

Chromatic aberration

Controls the amount of chromatic aberration on each color channel (red, green and blue).

A higher number means that this color channel is less affected by the effect. 1 means the color channel is completely unaffected by the effect.

Combine the values to get interesting results, like Red 0.40, Green 0.20, Blue 0.00.

Instead of putting all three values to something higher than 0, simply decrease the Displacement amount. In other words, try to keep at least one of the three values to 0.

Edge behavior

Controls how the edge of the video reacts when affected by the effect.

None will show the composition's background color.

Repeat will repeat the left part of the video over to the right, the bottop over to the top, ...

Mirror repeat will mirror the edge over itself.

Edge repeat will extend the last pixel next to the edge until the point where it is affected by the effect. Recommended.

Rotation

Rotates the whole effect. Not the content under.

Audio-reactive group

1. Make sure you have at least one layer with audio enabled in your composition;

2. Click the "Allow for audio-reactive features" button.
Once this button is clikced, any changes in the composition's audio will not affect the effect;

3. You can customise how the loudness of the song affects the effect by customizing its expressions.
To do that, select the "PXD A-R Layer > Effects > PXD Audio Keyframes > Slider" and open it until you see the word "value" appear in your timeline.
Activate "Show Post-Expression Graph" (little graph icon) next to "Enable Expression" (little equals icon).
Click on the "value" word. There, you will find a few pre-made expressions that you can enable by removing the // before them. Don't remove the // before the comments.
You can combine multiple of these expression by putting one inside of the other, replacing of the "value" word inside said expression.

A-R evolution & evolution multiplier

When turned on, let the loudness of the song drive the evolution of the effect.

A higher number means the louder the song, the faster the effect evolves.

A-R complexity & complexity divider

When turned on, let the loudness of the song drive the complexity of the effect.

A higher number means the louder the song, the less the effect is complex.

A-R displacement and displacement divider

When turned on, let the loudness of the song drive the displacement amount of the effect.

A higher number means the louder the song, the less the content underneath is displaced.

A-R partially disable and disable multiplier

For this to work, you needd to have the Partially disable enabled.

When turned on, let the loudness of the song drive how much of the image is unaffected by the effect.

A higher number means the louder the song, the more of the content underneath is affected.

Wiggles

What does a wiggle do?

When you activate a wiggle, the property it is referencing is going to randomly change, by a certain amount, at a certain speed.
How much?
By the amplitude amount.
At what speed?
By the frequency amount.

Extra effects

To activate any of these effects, simply click on the empty square next to their name, where an "fx" should appear.

All of those are basic After Effects effects. Using them is very straightforward.

FAQ

Clicking "Disrupt your pixels!" creates the layers but doesn't apply the effects.

This shouldn't happen. Please contact me via Instagram at @glypse.design or email at hello@glypse.design. In the meantime, here is a quick fix:

1. Copy the three animation presets files (meaning the Pixel Disruptor ScriptUI, MAP X, MAP Y and Content effects files) from your Pixel Disrutpor in Motion download folder;

2. Paste them inside the User Presets folder inside your documents folder:

macOS: /Documents/Adobe/After Effects <YEAR>/User Presets

Windows: Program Files\Adobe\Adobe After Effects <YEAR>\Support Files\User Presets\

3. Launch After Effects;

4. Inside the "Effects & Presets" panel, search "PXD";

5. Select each of the three layers generated and apply them their respective effect, one by one.

The "Allow for audio-reactive features" doesn't work properly.

This might be because you are using an older version of After Effects and/or in a different language. This is most likely an easy fix; contact me via Instagram at @glypse.design or email at hello@glypse.design with your After Effects version and language.
